Though it may seem as though donation of clothes to third-world countries are beneficial to the people, it actually ruins the economy. 70% of the clothing donated ends up in Africa, East Africa is also already in proposal try to ban second-hand clothing from entering their countries. These donated clothes causes a disruption in the natural economic cycle, instead of buying clothes from the locals, the people choose to buy clothes from the second-hand sellers. Most of the money not going back to community but the pockets of the people who pay for the bundle of clothes. Effects of a Bad Economy

The effects of a bad economy are recessions result in higher unemployment, lower wages and incomes, and lost opportunities more generally. Education, private capital investments, and economic opportunity are all likely to suffer in the current downturn, and the effects will be long-lived.
